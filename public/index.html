Search the brain

Model-free retrieval — returns the cited records by relevance, or nothing if the brain can't ground it. (The answer is written by your connected AI tool — see Connect.)

Ingest knowledge

Paste text, CSV, or JSON. It becomes scoped, retrievable knowledge your agents can ground on.

Sources in the brain

Real per-source record counts you can see (scope-filtered).

Approval queue

Actions an agent proposed. Nothing executes until a human approves — the governance gate.

Settings

Change the backend, retrieval, and keys without editing files. Saved to .env; takes effect on restart.

Amazon S3 Vectors (BYO — your own AWS)

For security, the bucket + index (where your data is stored) are set only via comb init / .env, never over the network — so no remote caller can redirect your data. Region is a safe preference.

Connect your AI tool

Comb is model-free — the answering happens in your AI tool (Claude, Cursor, Copilot) over MCP. One command connects it:

npm i -g open-company-brain comb install claude # or cursor · vscode · claude-code · windsurf

Then ask your agent: "search the brain for the refund policy." It calls search_brain and writes the answer from the cited records — or refuses if there's no grounding.

This console is the operator surface: ingest, configure, and approve actions. The conversation lives in your AI tool.
